Hello, its me, the ever-elusive Nashville native (yes, we still exist!) and I am thrilled to see how much the city is growing and changing. It has morphed into a hip, southern metropolis and I feel so fortunate to have witnessed it. For a long time, I only knew two things, that I loved my city and I wanted to make it a better place. It wasn't until I started coding that I felt like I had the tools to make an impact. For me, software development combines everything I love about learning and problem solving. It fuels my imagination and brings order to chaos and it is always pushing me learn more and seek a deeper understanding. Coming from the service industry, I am used to a certain level of camaraderie and I was pleasantly surprised to find that I wouldn't be deprived of that rich, engaging team environment. We have a saying in my house, "I can do all things through Google who strengthens me". There's nothing like the power of the internet combined with a can-do attitude. I would know. It has taken me far. Thanks to my experience at NSS, I went from learning what an h1 tag was through online tutorials to fast-paced, real-world group projects built on the foundational knowledge I gained in class. Learning to code has altered the way I think and has changed how I approach problems not just in programming but in life as well. It has provided me the opportunity to become a force for good in my community and for that I will be forever grateful.
